The 5 expensive mistakes

Where the $60,000 actually goes.

1
Buying a bot, not a system
The mistake — You buy a single AI tool that does one trick — a website chatbot, a note-taker, a scheduler — living on its own island, disconnected from everything else.
What it costs — It can't see your books, your patients, or your calendar, so it can't actually do much. Novelty wears off, logins go unused, and you've paid for a gadget.
✓
The fix — Treat AI as one assistant connected to the systems you already run — not a pile of disconnected apps.
2
Nobody owns it after launch
The mistake — The tool gets set up once, everyone claps, and then… no one is responsible for keeping it working.
What it costs — Software changes. Staff change. Within weeks the assistant gives stale answers or silently fails, and trust evaporates. “We tried AI, it didn't work” usually means “nobody maintained it.”
✓
The fix — AI needs an owner and ongoing monitoring — the same way you'd never install a security system and never check it again.
3
Going for the moonshot, not the foot in the door
The mistake — Trying to “transform the whole practice” in one giant, six-figure project.
What it costs — Big projects stall, blow budgets, and create so much disruption that staff revolt before you see a single result.
✓
The fix — Start small and connected. Prove the value in one office, with one assistant, then expand. The win pays for the next step.

The 5 expensive mistakes · continued

The two that hit healthcare hardest.

4
Ignoring your EHR and compliance reality
The mistake — Picking a shiny consumer AI tool that has no idea your world runs on an EHR and is bound by patient-privacy rules.
What it costs — Either it can't touch the systems that matter — or, worse, it touches them in ways that create compliance risk.
✓
The fix — The right setup treats your EHR as a first-class connection and is built around how healthcare actually handles data.
5
Buying a “frozen” agent that's obsolete in a year
The mistake — Paying once for an AI “agent” and assuming it's done forever.
What it costs — AI capabilities change every few months. A frozen, one-time build ages like milk — and you're back where you started, writing another check.
✓
The fix — Don't buy a frozen agent. Buy setup + ongoing adaptation, so it keeps pace as the tools and rules evolve.
Notice the pattern
Every mistake is a version of the same thing — disconnected, unowned, and left to go stale. So is every fix, in reverse.

What “done right” looks like

The 5-connection setup.

Instead of a pile of disconnected gadgets — one AI assistant wired into the five systems that run your day.

1
Your books — QuickBooks (desktop or online)
Pull last month's numbers, flag overdue invoices, prep month-end.
2
Microsoft 365 — email, docs, calendar
Draft replies, schedule, and find the right file without digging.
3
Your CRM — HubSpot, Salesforce, or ConnectWise
Log activity, follow up with leads, keep records current.
4
Your EHR — the system your practice actually lives in
Work within patient-privacy rules, not around them.
5
Your key line-of-business app — the one your team uses most
Automate the busywork in the tool you live in all day.
+ Ongoing monitoring
So it keeps working as everything around it changes.

Now a request like “pull last month's numbers” or “draft the patient follow-up” actually works — because the assistant can finally see the whole picture.

The bottom line
You don't need a $200K transformation. You need one connected assistant, set up right and kept right. That's the difference between a $60K lesson and a system that pays for itself.
